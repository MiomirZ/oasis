D'YOU KNOW WHAT I MEAN?

Oasis was an English rock band formed in Manchester in 1991. The band consisted of brothers Liam Gallagher on vocals and Noel Gallagher on guitar, along with Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs on guitar, Paul "Guigsy" McGuigan on bass, and Tony McCarroll on drums. Oasis quickly rose to fame in the mid-1990s with their debut album, "Definitely Maybe," which became the fastest-selling debut album in British history at the time. Their follow-up album, "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?" released in 1995, featured the hit singles "Wonderwall" and "Don't Look Back in Anger," and propelled the band to international superstardom. Throughout their career, Oasis became known for their larger-than-life personalities, sibling rivalries, and controversial behavior. Despite their internal conflicts, they released several successful albums, including "Be Here Now," "Standing on the Shoulder of Giants," and "Heathen Chemistry."

HALF THE WORLD AWAY

Oasis was a hugely popular and influential band during their heyday in the 1990s. They were one of the biggest bands in the world at the time, with a massive following of fans across the globe. Their second album, "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?", released in 1995, became a cultural phenomenon and sold over 22 million copies worldwide, making it one of the best-selling albums of all time. The album featured hit singles such as "Wonderwall," "Don't Look Back in Anger," and "Champagne Supernova," which helped to solidify Oasis' place in music history. The band's popularity was also reflected in their live performances, with Oasis consistently selling out massive venues such as Wembley Stadium in London, Maine Road in their hometown of Manchester, and of course Knebworth, where they played for 125,000 people two nights in a row. That is still to this day the biggest crowd in Knebworth's history.

Their concerts were known for their energetic and passionate performances, with fans singing along to every word of their songs. In addition to their commercial success, Oasis' influence on music and culture cannot be overstated. They were leaders of the Britpop movement in the UK, which had a significant impact on the music industry and popular culture at the time. Many bands that followed in their wake cited Oasis as a major influence on their music, and the band's legacy continues to inspire new generations of musicians. Overall, Oasis was a massive band that had a significant impact on the music industry and popular culture during their time together. Their music and influence continue to be celebrated and revered by fans around the world.

ROCK 'N' ROLL STAR

The band members were infamous for their partying, drug use, and larger-than-life personalities both on and offstage. Liam and Noel Gallagher, in particular, were known for their tumultuous relationship, which often played out in the media. They were known to engage in public fights, both verbally and physically, which added to their image as rebellious and unpredictable rock stars. The band's wild lifestyle often led to negative headlines in the press, with stories of drug use, alcohol-fueled binges, and outrageous behavior. In interviews, the band members were often unapologetic about their behavior, with Noel once famously stating, "I live for now, not for tomorrow. That's my motto." Despite their reputation, the band members were also known for their loyal friendships and dedication to their fans. They would often take time to meet and greet fans, sign autographs, and pose for photos, cementing their status as icons of the Britpop era.

The brothers who were the driving force behind the band Oasis, have been open about their past struggles with drug abuse. In the early days of the band, Liam and Noel's drug use was fueled by their newfound fame and rock 'n' roll lifestyle. They were often seen partying and indulging in drugs and alcohol, with Liam once stating in an interview, "I don't do cocaine to party, I do it to work." In a 2015 interview with Rolling Stone, Noel said, "When I was doing cocaine, I was fucking miserable, and I couldn't write anything, and I couldn't be creative, and I was a fucking nightmare to live with." Overall, the lifestyle of Oasis was one of excess, rebellion, and rock 'n' roll glamour, which helped to define their music and image as one of the most influential bands of their time.
