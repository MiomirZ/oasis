MEMBERS

LIAM GALLAGHER

Liam Gallagher is a British musician and songwriter, born on September 21, 1972, in Manchester, England. He is best known as the lead vocalist of the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he formed in 1991 with his older brother Noel Gallagher. Liam's distinctive singing style and on-stage persona, characterized by his swagger, attitude, and confrontational behavior, helped propel Oasis to international stardom during the 1990s Britpop era. The band's debut album, "Definitely Maybe," released in 1994, became the fastest-selling debut album in UK history at the time, and established them as one of the most important acts of their generation.

Over the next decade, Oasis released several critically acclaimed and commercially successful albums, including "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?" and "Be Here Now," and enjoyed a fierce but often tumultuous rivalry with fellow Britpop band Blur. In 2009, following years of infighting and public feuds between Liam and Noel, Oasis disbanded. Liam went on to form a new band, Beady Eye, and released two albums before disbanding in 2014. He then launched a successful solo career, releasing his debut solo album, "As You Were," in 2017, which debuted at number one in the UK charts. Throughout his career, Liam has remained a colorful and controversial figure, known for his outspoken views, heavy drinking, and occasional run-ins with the law. Despite this, he is widely regarded as one of the greatest rock vocalists of his generation, and his music continues to resonate with fans around the world.

NOEL GALLAGHER

Noel Gallagher is a British musician, songwriter, and producer, born on May 29, 1967, in Manchester, England. He is best known as the lead guitarist, primary songwriter, and occasional vocalist of the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he formed with his younger brother Liam Gallagher in 1991. Noel's songwriting talents and musical vision were key factors in Oasis' success, as he wrote many of the band's biggest hits, including "Wonderwall," "Don't Look Back in Anger," and "Champagne Supernova." His songwriting style, which blended classic rock influences with catchy pop hooks and soaring melodies, helped define the sound of the Britpop era.

Over the course of Oasis' career, Noel released several critically acclaimed and commercially successful albums, including "Definitely Maybe," "(What's the Story) Morning Glory?" and "Be Here Now." He also produced several of the band's albums and went on to produce albums for other artists. In 2009, following years of infighting and public feuds with his brother Liam, Noel left Oasis to form his own band, Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds. The band has released several successful albums, including their eponymous debut album in 2011, and "Chasing Yesterday" in 2015. Throughout his career, Noel has been recognized as one of the greatest songwriters and guitarists of his generation, and has won numerous awards for his contributions to music. His music continues to inspire and influence musicians around the world.

BONEHEAD

Bonehead, whose real name is Paul Arthurs, is a British musician, born on June 23, 1965, in Manchester, England. He is best known as the rhythm guitarist of the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he co-founded with Liam and Noel Gallagher in 1991. Bonehead's steady rhythm guitar playing was an essential component of Oasis' sound, providing a solid foundation for the band's anthemic melodies and catchy choruses. He played on all of Oasis' albums up until the band's fourth studio album, "Standing on the Shoulder of Giants," released in 2000. In addition to his role as a guitarist, Bonehead also served as a songwriter and producer for Oasis, co-writing several tracks with Noel Gallagher, including "Some Might Say" and "Don't Look Back in Anger."

Bonehead left Oasis in 1999, citing personal reasons, but remained on good terms with the band and continued to contribute to their music in various capacities. After leaving Oasis, he formed a new band, The Vortex, and has also collaborated with other artists, including Paul Weller and Ian Brown. Throughout his career, Bonehead has been widely respected for his contributions to Oasis' music and his influence on the Britpop scene of the 1990s. He remains an important figure in the Manchester music scene and continues to perform and record music to this day.

GUIGSY

Guigsy, whose real name is Paul McGuigan, is a British musician, born on May 9, 1971, in Manchester, England. He is best known as the bassist of the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he co-founded with Liam and Noel Gallagher in 1991. Guigsy's bass playing provided the backbone for Oasis' sound, anchoring the band's driving rhythms and catchy melodies. He played on all of Oasis' albums up until their third studio album, "Be Here Now," released in 1997. In addition to his role as a bassist, Guigsy also served as a songwriter for Oasis, co-writing several tracks with Noel Gallagher, including "Fade Away" and "Digsy's Dinner." Guigsy left Oasis in 1999, citing personal reasons, but remained on good terms with the band and continued to contribute to their music in various capacities. After leaving Oasis, he retired from the music industry and focused on his personal life. Throughout his career, Guigsy was respected for his contributions to Oasis' music and his understated but crucial role in the band's success.

He remains a beloved figure among Oasis fans and is remembered as a key member of one of the most influential bands of the 1990s. McGuigan occasionally performs as a DJ. He declined to appear in the 2004 Definitely Maybe DVD, although a polite letter explaining his reasons for doing so appears as a hidden extra and there is also a short segment featuring pundits giving their views on him.[5] He also declined to be interviewed for the 2016 Oasis: Supersonic documentary, though archive footage of him was used instead. McGuigan said of his playing style in 1995, "When I first started I just played up and down the top string of the bass. Come to think of it, that's what I still do now."

TONY MCCARROLL

Tony McCarroll is a British musician, born on June 25, 1971, in Manchester, England. He is best known as the original drummer of the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he co-founded with Liam and Noel Gallagher in 1991. McCarroll's powerful drumming style helped define the early sound of Oasis, providing the driving beats that propelled the band's rock anthems. He played on the band's debut album, "Definitely Maybe," which was released in 1994 and went on to become one of the best-selling albums of the decade. Despite his contributions to Oasis' early success, McCarroll was famously fired from the band in 1995, amidst tensions with the Gallagher brothers and allegations of substance abuse. He subsequently filed a lawsuit against the band, which was settled out of court in 1999.

After leaving Oasis, McCarroll pursued a career in music and released his memoir, "Oasis: The Truth," in 2010, which detailed his experiences in the band and the events that led to his departure. On the last day of April 1995, McCarroll's departure was announced and he was replaced by Alan White, who remained until 2004. Oasis producer Owen Morris said of McCarroll, "Tony was quiet and always polite to me, but seemed out of his depth…so I think Tony did well to survive as long as he did in Oasis". Morris described McCarroll's drumming style as "extremely basic", but with timing and tempo that were "almost autistically perfect". Throughout his career, McCarroll has remained a beloved figure among Oasis fans, who credit him with helping to create the band's signature sound. Despite the acrimony that surrounded his departure, he continues to be remembered as an important member of one of the most influential bands of the 1990s.

ALAN WHITE

Alan White is a British drummer, born on May 26, 1972, in County Durham, England. He is best known for his work as the drummer for the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he joined in 1995 following the departure of Tony McCarroll. White's powerful and versatile drumming style helped define the sound of Oasis during their peak years in the late 1990s, and he played on several of the band's most successful albums, including "Be Here Now" and "Standing on the Shoulder of Giants." In addition to his work with Oasis, White has also played with several other notable musicians and bands, including Paul Weller, The Who, and Tom Jones. He has been praised for his technical proficiency and his ability to adapt his playing style to suit a wide range of musical genres.

White left Oasis in 2004, following the band's tumultuous recording sessions for their sixth studio album, "Don't Believe the Truth." He subsequently formed his own band, Alan White & The White House, and has continued to work as a session musician and producer. On the other hand, Oasis producer Owen Morris described White as "essentially a jazz drummer" who "was always shuffling away on his snare [...] and never hitting the basic back beats in a big dumb rock and roll way", feeling that he did not understand Oasis' sound as well as McCarroll. At the time of his departure White was the longest-serving member in Oasis beside the Gallaghers (he was later passed by Gem Archer and Andy Bell) and passed through thick and thin with the brothers despite being struck down several times with bouts of alleged tendinitis during his later years with the band.

ANDY BELL

Andy Bell is a British musician, born on August 11, 1970, in Cardiff, Wales. He is best known for his work as the bassist and guitarist for the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he joined in 1999 following the departure of founding member Paul "Guigsy" McGuigan. Bell's addition to Oasis marked a significant shift in the band's sound, as he brought with him a more psychedelic and experimental style of playing. He played on several of the band's later albums, including "Heathen Chemistry" and "Don't Believe the Truth," and was credited with helping to expand Oasis' musical horizons. While with Oasis, Bell regularly made songwriting contributions to the band. On Heathen Chemistry, Bell wrote the song "A Quick Peep". His song "Thank You For the Good Times" was featured as the B-side of "Stop Crying Your Heart Out" from the same album. On Don't Believe The Truth, he wrote "Turn Up the Sun" (which also opened every live show on that Oasis tour) and "Keep the Dream Alive". On Dig Out Your Soul, he wrote "The Nature of Reality" Prior to joining Oasis, Bell was the frontman for the British shoegaze band Ride, which he co-founded in 1988. He also played in several other bands throughout the 1990s, including Hurricane #1 and The Hurricane Party.

After Oasis disbanded in 2009, Bell went on to form his own band, Beady Eye, with fellow former Oasis members Liam Gallagher, Gem Archer, and Chris Sharrock. He later joined the band Ride for a reunion tour in 2014 and has continued to perform and record as a solo artist. Throughout his career, Bell has been praised for his musical versatility and his contributions to some of the most influential British rock bands of the past few decades. He remains an important figure in the British music scene and continues to be remembered as one of the key members of Oasis' later years.

GEM ARCHER

Gem Archer is a British musician, born on December 7, 1966, in Durham, England. He is best known for his work as the guitarist and occasional songwriter for the iconic British rock band Oasis, which he joined in 1999 following the departure of founding member Paul "Bonehead" Arthurs. Archer's addition to Oasis marked a significant shift in the band's sound, as he brought with him a more melodic and pop-oriented style of playing. He played on several of the band's later albums, including "Heathen Chemistry," "Don't Believe the Truth," and "Dig Out Your Soul." Prior to joining Oasis, Archer was a member of several other notable British bands, including Heavy Stereo and The Creation. He has also worked as a session musician for a variety of artists, including The Charlatans, Cast, and Beady Eye.

After Oasis disbanded in 2009, Archer went on to form his own band, Noel Gallagher's High Flying Birds, with fellow former Oasis member Noel Gallagher. He also continued to work as a session musician and produced music for several other artists. Throughout his career, Archer has been praised for his musical versatility and his contributions to some of the most influential British rock bands of the past few decades. He remains an important figure in the British music scene and continues to be remembered as one of the key members of Oasis' later years.
